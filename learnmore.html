    • The art of war in Vietnam history

    Throughout of the entire history of Vietnam, they have defeated many strong Empires and country from Mongolia Empire, China to France and United State. To won againts those invaders, tactics play a very important role in the war so lets summarize all the tactics history of this country. One of the most useful tactics in dynastic period in Vietnam was putting stakes under the river and lurring them to the river wait the water to fall down and destroy enemies naval and one of the significant battle using this was battle of Bạch Đằng river in 1228 made thousands of Yuan armies dead or captured

    Another tactics was Empty garden, when army destroy food and properties and move the citizen away so that when the enemies came they didnt have any food and supply from the destroyed place and this was used in 1258 againts Mongol Empire made the enemies suffered from food shortage and play an important role to win the battle. And the most used tactic strategy in vietnam was guerilla, when facing much more stronger enemies expecially againts United State in Vietnam war, Vietnamese soldier can not face them directly, instead they ambush the army from the bush or the forest and even the people who fought for america during this war have to admit that they were very scared when looking at any bush or trees because they dont know if they can speak vietnamese and shot AK-47

    Hồ Chí Minh on the road finding independence for Vietnam

    On 6 May 1911, in Nhà Rồng harbour, a young person name Nguyễn Tất Thành get on the Amiral Latouche-Tréville ship with the job being the chef helper with a wish to find a way to make Vietnam become independence. The ship arrived in France on 5 July 1911 then the ship arrived to Dunkirk and return to France on mid-September and here he applied to a Colonial school but got rejected so he deciđe to visit more countries in 1911

    Then he returned to France from around 1917-1919 and in here he has been publishing newspaper for Vietnamese independence and their civil right under a name called Nguyễn Ái Quốc. In treaty of Versailles, Nguyễn Ái Quốc send the Demands of the Annamite People outlining eight points to achieve basic rights for the Vietnamese people under French colonial rule although it was rejected by global powers. In 7/1920, he read The Preliminary Draft of Theses on the Colonial Question by Lenin and till that time he has finally found the ideology to make Vietnam become independent from France

    In the later years, he come to Soviet and China to study about Marxism-Leninism and organize the Vietnam Revolutionary Youth Association publishing newspapers called Thanh Niên newspapers. In 1930, he united the 3 Communism party in Vietnam marking a breakthrough in the road to independent. He later returned to Vietnam in 1941 with the name Hồ Chí Minh founded Việt Minh front and led the August Revolution in 1945, declearing independence for Vietnam after many years of colonization
